## 1. Problem statement

Trinity Family Health, a 7-clinic primary-care network in DFW, is losing an
estimated $1–2M per year to two linked operational failures: patient no-shows
that leave provider time unbilled, and poorly balanced schedules that leave
some providers overbooked while others sit idle.

The figure is currently a felt estimate, not a defensible number. Leadership
cannot point to its source, size it by cause, or say which fixes would recover
the most for the least cost.

## 2. The decision this project informs

The sponsor will present a funding request to the board within 90 days for a
**phased program of interventions** — not a single product purchase. This
project produces the business case that request rests on: the size of the loss,
a ranked set of interventions, the cost and expected return of each, and a
method for measuring whether they worked.

## 3. Objectives

1. Quantify the annual cost of no-shows and idle capacity, with a stated method
   a board can scrutinise.
2. Identify the drivers of both, by cause and by clinic.
3. Recommend a ranked, phased set of interventions that fits the budget ceiling.
4. Model the ROI of each intervention, with sensitivity analysis and break-even.
5. Define the KPIs and measurement plan that will show, within two quarters,
   whether the program is working.

## 4. Constraints

| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Year-one budget ceiling | ~$600,000 across the whole program |
| Required payback | Within 18 months |
| Board presentation | Within 90 days |
| Success visibility | Measurable within 2 quarters of launch |
| Key adversary | A senior physician will contest any recommendation in the board room; every number must survive hostile review |

## 5. In scope

- Cost of no-shows and idle provider capacity, quantified and defensible.
- Driver analysis by cause and clinic.
- A ranked, costed, phased intervention portfolio under the budget ceiling.
- A measurement plan with KPIs, owners, and targets.
- The four clinics with sufficient data history, plus whatever the other three
  can support.

## 6. Out of scope

**These are excluded by sponsor decision. Requests to add them mid-project are
declined by reference to this charter.**

- **Billing, coding, and claim denials.** Separate problem, separate team.
- **Purchasing or replacing the scheduling software.** This project optimises
  use of existing systems; a platform migration is a separate capital decision.
- **Clinical protocols or care standards.** This project does not advise on how
  medicine is practised.
- **The two newest clinics** (under six months of data). Deferred to a later
  phase.

## 8. Success criteria

- The cost of no-shows and idle capacity is quantified with a method the board
  accepts.
- Every recommended intervention traces to evidence, a cost, and an expected
  return.
- The measurement plan would let leadership judge the program within two
  quarters.
- The business case survives hostile review from a clinical stakeholder.
